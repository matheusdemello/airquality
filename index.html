Quick Guide

Do's & Don'ts

Simple guidelines for creating a healthier home environment.

Do

  • Seal windows and doors with natural wool-felt strips
  • Keep indoor temperature above 18°C
  • Ventilate briefly at Golden Hours when air is cleanest
  • Perform weekly inspections of felt seals
  • Use renewable, locally-sourced materials

Don't

  • Use synthetic foam or rubber seals (petroleum-based)
  • Allow condensation buildup that promotes mold
  • Ventilate during morning rush hours (8AM)
  • Use materials that off-gas VOCs
  • Ignore seal degradation over time
Research

Evidence & Messaging

Key claims and rationale backed by WHO and UNICEF research.

Effective sealing with felt strips and maintaining a stable temperature ensures the home stays above the 18°C threshold recommended for protecting respiratory health (WHO 2018).

Sealing drafts with wool-felt can reduce heat loss by up to 25%, a critical factor for families facing energy poverty (ARIS 2020).

"We ventilate at 2:00 PM because the air is 30% cleaner than at 8:00 AM" (UNICEF 2022). This timing maximizes fresh air while minimizing pollution exposure.

We connect the checklist to the Yurt tradition. Framing the felt strips and Kelt (lattice) ventilation as a modern evolution of the Kerege (yurt wall) honors Kyrgyz heritage, making the health intervention feel like an act of cultural pride rather than an external instruction.

"Just as the Tunduk (roof hole) of the yurt was opened during the sunny heat of the day to let out the smoke, we open our modern windows when the sun is highest to let our 'Nest' breathe."

Framework

The Seven C's Strategy

A comprehensive communication framework for the Ak-shumkar Nest campaign.

1

Command Attention

The Ak-shumkar (White Falcon) symbol represents a guardian of the household, commanding attention by framing the home as a "Nest" requiring intentional construction.

2

Clarify Message

A three-step Nest Checklist: Seal, Isolate, and Ventilate. This addresses the dual challenge of preventing cold air infiltration while ensuring proper air exchange.

3

Communicate Benefit

Primary benefit: a child free from respiratory diseases. Secondary benefit: reduced heat loss means lower household expenditure on fuel.

4

Consistency

Bilingual (Kyrgyz/Russian) messaging with repeated use of the "Nest Checklist" across clinics and schools turns recommendations into permanent social norms.

5

Cater to Heart & Head

Science provides the proof; tradition provides the motivation. Connecting felt seals to Yurt heritage makes health intervention an act of cultural pride.

6

Create Trust

Integrating local knowledge with international expertise from UNICEF and WHO creates a "Gold Standard" of care that increases adoption in rural contexts.

7

Call to Action

The Weekly Nest Check transforms passive knowledge into an active, life-saving habit through regular seal inspections and short-burst ventilation.
